The Timeless Pyramids

The Pyramids of Giza, particularly the Great Pyramid, are iconic symbols of ancient Egyptian engineering and ambition. Built during the Old Kingdom around 2600 BCE, these structures served as tombs for pharaohs, designed to ensure their safe passage to the afterlife. The precision involved in their construction, including aligning the pyramids with celestial bodies, highlights the Egyptians' advanced understanding of mathematics, astronomy, and engineering.

The Great Pyramid of Giza, originally standing at 146 meters, was the tallest man-made structure in the world for over 3,800 years. Its limestone casing and intricate internal chambers demonstrate both aesthetic and functional ingenuity.
